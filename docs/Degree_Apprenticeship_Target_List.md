# Degree Apprenticeship Target List

Built for Dan Mehretab | Updated 3 July 2026

## Ranking logic

This list is ordered by total career value, not just starting salary.

I weighted each employer using:

- starting salary or published salary signal
- salary growth after qualification
- brand value
- probability of moving into high-paying work later
- quality of training
- relevance to MARS, Meridian and the Decision Systems Portfolio
- scarcity and competitiveness
- technical depth
- commercial exposure
- long-term optionality

Salary notes are deliberately cautious. Many employers do not publish apprentice salaries clearly, and some change by location. Where exact salary is not public, use the ranking as a strategic signal, then confirm when the live vacancy opens.

## Best angle for your applications

Use this sentence everywhere:

> I build explainable decision systems that help people make better choices under uncertainty.

For banks:

> I built MARS to understand how market stress signals can be turned into an explainable risk recommendation, and Meridian to explore how limited capital can be allocated under uncertainty.

For consulting:

> My projects show how I approach ambiguous client problems: define the decision, structure the evidence, test the trade-offs and explain the recommendation.

For aerospace / defence / engineering:

> I am interested in systems where decisions need to be safe, explainable and robust under changing conditions.

For law:

> My portfolio is evidence that I can handle complex information, identify what matters, reason clearly and explain a recommendation in plain English.

## Application timing

As of 3 July 2026, many 2026 intakes are closed. The serious move is to prepare now for 2027.

Expected pattern:

- July-August 2026: build evidence, CV, LinkedIn, interview answers and application tracker.
- September-November 2026: many finance, professional services and engineering schemes reopen.
- November 2026-January 2027: engineering, aerospace, defence and infrastructure schemes often open or continue.
- Apply within the first week when possible. Many schemes close early once enough applications arrive.
