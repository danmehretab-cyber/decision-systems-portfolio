Adaptive Capital Allocation Engine

  • Commercial capital allocation

  • Outputs allocation, survivability and evidence gaps

Why this portfolio is different

The point is not that I built two notebooks. The point is that I can take ambiguous real-world problems and turn them into systems with inputs, constraints, tests and explainable outputs.

Problem solving

Both projects decompose vague decisions into measurable drivers, scoring logic and output recommendations.

Data-driven thinking

MARS uses forward-risk validation; Meridian uses scenario survivability, confidence and risk controls.

Commercial awareness

Meridian directly models capital allocation; MARS models financial exposure and risk control.

Engineering judgement

The public versions separate demos from raw research, archive messy notebooks and show limitations honestly.

30-second portfolio pitch

I built two Python decision systems. MARS detects market fragility and recommends risk exposure. Meridian allocates limited business capital across opportunities using confidence, risk and scenario testing. Together they show how I approach complex systems: define the decision, structure the data, model the trade-offs, test the downside and make the recommendation explainable.
